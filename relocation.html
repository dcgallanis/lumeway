What to expect

A timeline of what
needs to happen

8 weeks before

Plan and prepare

  • Research new city — neighborhoods, schools, healthcare providers
  • Get moving quotes from at least 3 licensed movers
  • Begin decluttering — donate, sell, or discard what you won't move
  • Notify employer of address change for payroll and tax purposes
4 weeks before

Notify and transfer

  • File change of address with USPS
  • Notify banks, credit cards, and subscription services
  • Transfer or request school records for children
  • Schedule utility disconnection and new service setup
Move week

Execute the move

  • Confirm moving company details and insurance coverage
  • Pack essentials bag for first night in new home
  • Document condition of old and new home with photos
  • Transfer prescriptions to new pharmacy
First 30 days

Establish yourself

  • Update driver's license and vehicle registration in new state
  • Register to vote in new location
  • Find new doctors, dentists, and specialists
  • Update homeowner's or renter's insurance

Critical deadlines

Don't miss these
time-sensitive steps

30 days
Driver's license update

Most states require you to update your driver's license within 30-60 days of establishing residency. Check your new state's DMV.

30 days
Vehicle registration

Register your vehicle in your new state within 30-90 days. Driving with out-of-state plates too long can result in fines.

Tax year
State tax residency

You may owe taxes in both your old and new state for the year you move. Keep records of your move date for tax filing purposes.

Before move
USPS mail forwarding

Set up mail forwarding before your move date. USPS forwards mail for up to 12 months but it's not a substitute for updating your address.

30 days
Voter registration

Register to vote in your new state. Deadlines vary — some states allow same-day registration, others require 30 days in advance.

Varies
Professional licenses

If you hold a professional license (nurse, teacher, contractor, etc.), check if it transfers or needs to be re-obtained in your new state.
