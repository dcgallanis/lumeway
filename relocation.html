    What to expect

    A timeline of what
    needs to happen

    6–8 weeks before

    Research and prepare

    • Research your new area: cost of living, schools, healthcare providers, neighborhoods
    • Give notice to your landlord or list your home for sale
    • Start transferring medical records and requesting prescription transfers
    • Notify your employer if working remotely and changing states (tax implications)
    2–4 weeks before

    Start the paperwork

    • Set up mail forwarding through USPS (do this at least 2 weeks early)
    • Call your auto insurance company — rates change by state and ZIP code
    • Research your new state's requirements for driver's license and vehicle registration
    • Schedule utility setup at your new address and cancellation at your current one
    First week after

    Establish yourself

    • Get your new driver's license and register your vehicles (check your state's deadline — some are as short as 10 days)
    • Register to vote at your new address
    • Find a new primary care doctor, dentist, and any specialists
    • Update your address with banks, credit cards, insurance, and subscriptions
    First 1–3 months

    Settle in

    • Establish residency for tax purposes (important if you moved mid-year)
    • Update your will and estate documents — state laws differ significantly
    • Enroll kids in school and transfer records
    • Find local community resources: library, recreation, places of worship, support groups

    Critical deadlines

    Don't miss these
    time-sensitive steps

    10–90 days
    Driver's license transfer

    This varies wildly by state. Some (like Florida and Georgia) require transfer within 30 days. Others give you 90 days. A few require it within 10 days. Check your new state immediately.

    30 days
    Vehicle registration

    Most states require vehicle registration within 30 days of establishing residency. You may also need a new state inspection or emissions test.

    30 days
    Voter registration update

    Update your registration at vote.org or your local election office. You'll need to re-register in your new state — your old registration doesn't transfer.

    Varies
    State income tax residency

    If you move mid-year, you may owe income tax in both states. Rules for establishing residency vary — some states count days, others look at where your "domicile" is.

    60 days
    Insurance updates

    Update homeowner's or renter's insurance for your new address. Auto insurance rates change by location — failing to update could void your coverage.

    Before enrollment
    School registration

    Contact your new school district early. You'll need immunization records, previous transcripts, and proof of residency. IEP/504 plans should be transferred as well.
