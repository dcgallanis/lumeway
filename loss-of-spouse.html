Loss of a spouse

You don't have to figure
this out alone

Losing a spouse is devastating. And yet the world keeps asking things of you — paperwork, phone calls, deadlines. Here's a clear guide to what needs to happen and when.

What needs to happen

The first 30 days

Day 1–3

Obtain death certificates

Order at least 10–12 certified copies from the funeral home or vital records office. You'll need them for banks, insurance, Social Security, and more. Each institution will want an original.

Day 1–7

Notify Social Security

Call 1-800-772-1213. If your spouse received benefits, payments must stop. You may be eligible for a one-time $255 death benefit and ongoing survivor benefits.

Day 1–7

Contact life insurance companies

Locate all policies and file claims promptly. You'll need the death certificate and policy numbers. Benefits are typically paid within 30–60 days of a complete claim.

Week 1–2

Locate the will and contact an estate attorney

Find the original will (not a copy). Contact a probate attorney to understand next steps — most states require the estate to go through probate if assets exceed a threshold.

Week 2–4

Notify financial institutions

Contact banks, investment accounts, and credit card companies. Transfer or close joint accounts. Freeze or cancel cards in your spouse's name to prevent fraud.

Within 60 days

Elect COBRA health insurance

If you were covered under your spouse's employer health plan, you have 60 days to elect COBRA continuation coverage. Missing this deadline means losing coverage.

Within 60 days

Apply for survivor benefits

Social Security survivor benefits don't back-pay beyond 6 months — apply as soon as possible. You may also be eligible for pension survivor benefits from your spouse's employer.

First tax year

File taxes as qualifying surviving spouse

You can file a joint return for the year your spouse died. For the next two years you may qualify for "qualifying surviving spouse" status with lower tax rates if you have dependent children.
