## Detailed Findings

### Claim 1 — COBRA Election Notice Deadline: 44 days (30 employer + 14 plan admin)
**Status: ✅ ACCURATE**

DOL rules confirmed: employer must notify plan administrator within 30 days of a qualifying event; the plan administrator then has 14 days to send the COBRA election notice to the qualified beneficiary. Total outer deadline = 44 days.

Note: cobrainsurance.com cited "within 45 days," which appears to be a simplified/rounded figure. The DOL-based 44-day figure (30 + 14) on the Lumeway site is more precise and accurate.

- Source: https://benefitslink.com/boards/topic/73659-cobra-election-notice-timeline/ — "outer deadline at 44 days from the loss of coverage"
- Source: https://www.dgb-online.com/blog/cobra-notification-of-termination-deadlines/ — employer 30-day rule confirmed

### Claim 4 — COBRA Maximum Duration: 18 months
**Status: ✅ ACCURATE**

Standard COBRA coverage lasts 18 months for employees and dependents who lose coverage due to termination or reduction in hours. Extensions to 29 or 36 months exist in specific disability/dependent circumstances but don't affect the standard claim.

- Source: https://www.cobrainsurance.com/kb/how-long-can-you-stay-on-cobra/ — "You may keep COBRA coverage for up to 18 months as an employee"

### Claim 5 — Marketplace Special Enrollment Period: 60 days from coverage end
**Status: ✅ ACCURATE**

Loss of employer-sponsored coverage triggers a 60-day SEP on HealthCare.gov and all state marketplaces. Additionally, if you have advance notice of coverage loss, the SEP window can begin up to 60 days *before* the qualifying event.

### Claim 6 — FMLA Job Protection: Up to 12 weeks
**Status: ✅ ACCURATE**

Federal FMLA continues to provide up to 12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected leave for eligible employees. No changes to the federal 12-week rule in 2026.

**Contextual note:** Washington State expanded its paid leave job protection rules starting January 1, 2026 (employers with 25+ employees), but this is a WA-specific program layered on top of FMLA — not a change to federal FMLA itself.

- Source: https://benely.com/the-complete-guide-to-us-family-and-medical-leave-rules-for-2026/ — "Eligible employees may be entitled to up to 12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected leave under FMLA"

### Claim 7 — SDI States: CA, HI, NJ, NY, RI
**Status: ✅ ACCURATE**

The five states with mandatory employer-provided short-term disability insurance (SDI/TDI/DBL) remain: California, Hawaii, New Jersey, New York, and Rhode Island. No new state added mandatory SDI in 2026.

**Contextual note worth monitoring:** Many additional states now have Paid Family and Medical Leave (PFML) programs (CO, CT, DE, DC, MA, ME, MD, MN, OR, WA, and others). PFML is different from traditional SDI, but the distinction may confuse readers. If the disability page doesn't already distinguish these, a brief note could help readers in non-SDI states understand they may still have some wage-replacement options through PFML.

- Source: EY 2026 state disability and PFML rates table

### Claim 9 — Social Security Claiming Ages: 62 reduced, 67 full, 70 max
**Status: ✅ ACCURATE**

Confirmed for 2026. For people born in 1960 or later — which now covers all people first becoming eligible for SS retirement benefits — the full retirement age is 67. Early claiming at 62 results in a permanent reduction (up to 30%). Delayed claiming up to age 70 earns the maximum benefit via delayed retirement credits (8% per year past FRA).

**2026-specific note:** Some news coverage about "FRA change in 2026" refers to 2026 being the first year all new early claimants (born 1964, turning 62) have an FRA of exactly 67. The gradual phase-in is complete — the site's claim of "67 = full" is now fully accurate for all new claimants going forward.

- Source: https://www.livenowfox.com — confirms 62, 67, 70 framework
- Source: https://finance.yahoo.com — "Maximum Social Security Benefits at Ages 62, 67 and 70 in 2026"

### Claim 10 — Medicare IEP: 7-month window starting 3 months before 65th birthday
**Status: ✅ ACCURATE**

Confirmed. The Initial Enrollment Period (IEP) begins 3 months before the month of your 65th birthday, includes your birthday month, and extends 3 months after — a total of 7 months.

- Source: https://www.kff.org — "your initial enrollment period for Part A and Part B begins three months before the month of your 65th birthday and ends three months after it"
- Source: https://www.kiplinger.com/retirement/medicare/turning-65-in-2026-how-to-sign-up-for-medicare

**Status: ✅ ACCURATE**

Confirmed via SSA Program Operations Manual. The SSA handbook states: *"full retirement age claims and survivor claims may be paid for up to six months retroactively."* (Note: disability claims can be retroactive up to 12 months; SSI and Medicare are not eligible for retroactive payments.)

### Claim 12 — 401k Early Withdrawal Has Penalties
**Status: ✅ ACCURATE**

The 10% early withdrawal penalty for 401(k) withdrawals before age 59½ remains in effect in 2026, in addition to ordinary income taxes.

**New 2026 development:** A new rule effective January 2026 allows penalty-free 401(k) withdrawals specifically for long-term care insurance premiums. This doesn't change the general claim that early withdrawals have penalties, but it's a new exception the site could optionally mention on the job-loss or retirement page.

- Source: https://www.wral.com — "Normally, withdrawing money from a 401(k) before age 59-and-a-half triggers a 10% early-withdrawal penalty"
- Source: https://www.cnbc.com/select/early-401k-withdrawal/

### Claim 13 — QDRO Required for Retirement Account Division in Divorce
**Status: ✅ ACCURATE**

A Qualified Domestic Relations Order (QDRO) remains required to divide ERISA-covered retirement accounts (401(k)s, pensions) in divorce without triggering tax penalties or early withdrawal fees. No changes to QDRO rules in 2026.

- Source: https://www.irs.gov/retirement-plans/plan-participant-employee/retirement-topics-qdro-qualified-domestic-relations-order
- Source: https://provinziano.com/blog/the-qdro-process-dividing-retirement-accounts-in-california/
